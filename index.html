What is a seltzer and why does Happy Dad do it differently?

  • First of all, seltzer is not alcohol, but hard seltzers are carbonated sodas spiked to the alcohol level of a light beer. Hard seltzers are made by adding a little sugar to carbonated water, which is then fermented by introducing yeast so that the sugars are converted into alcohol. The alcohol content of most spiked seltzers falls in the range of 4-6% alcohol by volume (ABV). Some can be as high as 12% ABV, which is the same amount as the standard five-ounce serving of wine. Virtually any type of spirit or alcoholic beverage can be used to make hard seltzers.
